# Slot Machine Psychology

The goal of this research is to investigate what features make slot machines addictive.

## Background

Slot machines bring in more revenue than all the other games in a casino combined, known as the "crack cocaine" of gambling. Players don't even play to win, they play to be in a trancelike state called the "machine zone" where they have no awareness or worries. Side Note: They also began renaming gambling to gaming to distance themselves from the negative connotations of gambling

## B.F. Skinner and Variable Rewards

Skinner Box experiment, where pigeons pecked at a button that gave them food. Pigeons who got food at a variable schedule pecked the button a lot more aggressively than the ones who got food at random intervals. **The anticipation of reward motivates behavior more than actually receiving the reward.** This works on humans as well.

## Addiction Engineering

Slot machines allow players to place massive amounts of bets very quickly. The variable reward cycle is a lot quicker than other games. The five elements of slot machine design are considered to be: Color, light, animation, sound, and space. There needs to be a balance for each of these elements so that everything is as seamless as possible.

- Equipment immediacy: Gambling is thrust upon visitors as they enter
- Players avoid expanse: Excess horizontal space, visible depth, vertical space
- Low stress lighting
- Dark and enclosed
- Sound only bothers players when it is bounced back at them
- Control the ambience with music, "players want to experience sound"
- Avoid visual overstimulation that is too bright
- Softly pixelate the screen to reduce glare
- Good feedback on clicks, like subtle click effects with unique vibrations
- Encourage players to keep playing, minimize the effort it takes to play again (kinda like how it's really easy to get trapped into scrolling reels)
- Digital Soundtrack to simulate cascading coins
- Service requests? If something's wrong give the player a place to complain
- Speed is key, increase play speed or maybe have options where players can increase the speed
- Increase level tiers as people play, second screens, visual effects that change the longer people play
- Add a bonus payout that happens after a certain number of spins with a visual cue
- High probability of desirable outcomes, celebrate wins that are loses (like 10 cent spins with 1 cent wins)
