## 1: Types of slot machines
There appears to be many types of slot machines
+ Classic Slots: I think this most closely resembles our final slot from the first warm up. Less flashy, simple 3 slots
+ Video Slots: Looks more like the ones shown last Thursday with more effects and appealing background, more features and small bonuses to engage user 
+ Progressive Slots: Every slot machine in a network plays towards one jackpot (winner takes all), similar to video slots 
+ Other types of slots include 3D, branded/themed, and specialty slots that all enhance the increased engagement of the video slots. 
  
## 2: Relevant information and terms
+ **RTP(return to player):** A percentage value that represents how much money the user should expect to gain. Example: 40% = $40 gains out of $100. 
+ **Volatility:** A value that helps a user make informed decisions on the nature of the payouts. Higher volatility means more sparse big payouts, while lower volatility means more frequent smaller payouts. A volatility in the middle would probably lead to longer user engagement. 
+ **Wilds:** Symbols in a slot machine that can act as substitutes for wins. 
+ **Scatters:** Symbols that don't necessarily need to match. Their main purpose is to give small bonuses like free spins and increase play time. 
+ **Bonus:** Different from bonuses granted by scatters, bonus symbols will trigger mini games and other things related adjacently to the theme/brand. 

## 3: Slot machine design and psychology
+ Colors matter for different aspects of the slot machines: blue backgrounds signal longer play times, red and green elements signal higher player confidence, gold and yellow symbols signal greater reward confidence and win reactions
+ More player base centered themes will also increase play time rather than randomly selected themes
+ Indicators like visual and audible ones will also increase positivity and confidence in players. 
+ Misses that are close to wins have similar responses to actual wins.
